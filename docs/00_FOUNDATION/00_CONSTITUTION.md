# Mission

To give every brand a clear, trustworthy answer to the question: "How do AI systems see us?"

AI Visibility Auditor exists to measure, explain, and improve how brands are represented, recommended, and reasoned about by AI systems.

# Vision

A world where brand visibility inside AI-mediated answers is as measurable, auditable, and improvable as visibility in traditional search — and where every organization, regardless of size or language, has access to that clarity.

# Core Principles

- Truth over convenience. The product reports what is actually observed, never what is comfortable to report.
- Trust is the product. Every feature, metric, and claim must be defensible and reproducible.
- Clarity over cleverness. Insights must be understandable by the people who need to act on them.
- The customer's outcome is the measure of success, not the sophistication of the method used to get there.
- Independence of judgment. The product's assessments are not for sale and are not influenced by who is being measured.

# Engineering Principles

- Every engine is independent. Capabilities are built, tested, and reasoned about in isolation from one another.
- Business logic is language-agnostic and portable. It does not depend on any single implementation language or runtime.
- The system is multilingual by design, not by later adaptation.
- Product quality has priority over development speed.
- Complexity is a cost. It is only accepted when it pays for itself in durable value.
- Avoid vendor lock-in. Dependence on any single external provider is treated as a risk to be managed, not a convenience to be embraced.

# Product Principles

- AI Visibility is the core product. All other capabilities exist to support, explain, or extend it.
- The frontend is only one consumer of the platform. The platform's value must not depend on any single interface.
- The product serves the person who must act on the answer, not merely the person who requested it.
- Every insight delivered must be explainable in terms a non-technical stakeholder can act upon.

# Architectural Principles

- The platform is a system of independent, composable engines, not a monolith of intertwined features.
- Interfaces between components are stable contracts; internal implementation is free to evolve.
- No single component is indispensable. The system is designed to tolerate the replacement of any one part.
- Operational cost is a first-class design constraint, not an afterthought.

# Decision Hierarchy

When principles are in tension, decisions are resolved in this order:

1. Trust and truthfulness of what is reported to the customer.
2. Product quality and correctness.
3. Long-term architectural integrity.
4. Cost and operational efficiency.
5. Speed of delivery.

A decision that wins on a lower priority but loses on a higher one is not accepted.

# Definition of Success

The product succeeds when a customer can, in a single sitting, understand how AI systems represent their brand, trust that representation as accurate, and know what action to take next — regardless of the customer's market, industry, or language.

# Out of Scope

This constitution does not define:

- Specific technologies, platforms, or vendors.
- Implementation details, architecture diagrams, or system designs.
- Timelines, roadmaps, or sprint-level decisions.
- Pricing, packaging, or go-to-market strategy.

These belong to other documents and may change; this document does not.

# Long-Term Vision

AI Visibility Auditor becomes the trusted, independent standard by which organizations understand and improve their standing in an AI-mediated world — expanding from measurement into guidance, and from a single product into a platform that other tools and teams build upon.
